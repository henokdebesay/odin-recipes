Spicy Red Lentils

Look for Ethiopian Berbere spice—a mixture of dried chiles, cloves, ginger, coriander, and allspice—at gourmet markets and specialty stores, or order it from americanspice.com. This stew recipe is not one to be missed.

Cooking time

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons canola oil
  • 2 cups chopped red onion
  • 1 tablespoon minced peeled fresh ginger
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 ½ tablespoons Berbere spice
  • 3 cups organic vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dried small red lentils
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ cup finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 4 cups hot cooked basmati rice

Instructions

  1. Purée onions in a blender, or finely chop.
  2. Sauté the onions until they are light brown, then add ½ of oil and berbere.
  3. Let simmer for 2 minutes before adding tomatoes.
  4. Add the shiro powder gradually, stirring briskly with a wooden spoon or wire whisk.
  5. Once mixed, add 1 ½ to 2 cups of water, dependent on how soft you want it.
  6. Add more berbere to taste. Stir well.
  7. Turn your heat down to medium and let the mixture thicken as it cooks.
  8. Let cook for 5 minutes.
  9. Serve with injera, rice, rolls or regular bread.
